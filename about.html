About

I'm Daniel Kelley. I grew up in Llanelli, in south Wales.

As a teenager I became one of the most prolific cyber criminals in the UK. I'm best known for my part in the 2015 TalkTalk breach, which cost the company around £77m and exposed the data of more than 150,000 customers. I hacked other organisations too, across the UK, US and Australia, and extorted some of them. In 2016 I pleaded guilty, and in 2019 I was sentenced to four years.

I don't write that to dramatise it. It is the most documented thing about me, so it may as well be here in my own words.

Since then I've spent my time on the other side of it. On bail and after release I reported thousands of vulnerabilities to the organisations that had them, worked with incident response teams and developers, and ranked in the top 15 of a major bug bounty programme. I went on to work as a security researcher, and I now run Cyberou, a threat-content agency for cybersecurity companies.

I write here about what I learned doing both, mostly about why organisations get breached and what actually closes the gap once a problem is known.
